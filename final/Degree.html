Curriculum & Length

During a four-year bachelor's degree in computer science, students take general education credits in English, math, science, business, and history, plus computer science courses. Some employers prefer professionals to have a broad general education and degree to demonstrate proficiency.

Is a Degree in Computer Science Still Worth it?

Yes, a computer science degree is generally still worth it in 2025, particularly for those seeking structured learning, networking opportunities, and a strong foundation for careers in the tech industry. While self-teaching and bootcamps can be alternative paths, a CS degree offers a comprehensive curriculum and the credibility that many employers value, especially for specialized roles or work visas.

Reasons Why The Degree Path is a Good Choice:

  • Strong job market: The demand for computer science professionals remains high, with numerous job openings projected in the coming years.
  • Structured learning: A CS degree provides a well-rounded education in fundamental concepts like algorithms, data structures, and programming languages.
  • Networking opportunities: University environments offer valuable networking opportunities with peers, professors, and potential employers.
  • Credibility and career prospects: A degree can open doors to various roles, from software engineering and data science to cybersecurity and AI, with potential for high salaries.
  • Foundation for specialization: While the tech landscape evolves rapidly, core CS principles remain relevant and provide a strong basis for specialization in emerging fields.
  • Higher earning potential: Computer science occupations tend to pay well, with a median wage significantly higher than the national average, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (.gov).
